MBA (Master of Business Administration) Course Details, Colleges, Admission Process, Entrance Exams, Eligibility, Campus Placements

MBA or Master of Business Administration is a 2-year post-graduate degree in management. It is an another popular course option for higher studies after BCA , a lot of BCA graduates take up an MBA course after their graduation to earn a management degree. It is an internationally-recognized degree designed to develop the skills required for careers in business and management. It prepares students for the corporate world.Some colleges include mandatory internships at a company or organization during the breaks in both the years. It is done to enhance the practical knowledge and skills of the students,which leads to concrete job opportunities after the course.

The basic curriculum of an MBA programme include subjects related to Marketing, Human Resource Management, Business Planning, Finance Management, etc.

Eligibility Criteria :

The eligibility criteria for an MBA course is qualifying graduation in any discipline from a recognised university.

Students must fulfill the following as well to be eligible to apply for an MBA entrance exam :

  • Minimum marks required: 50% aggregrate in Bachelor’s, for students belonging to SC/ST or PWD category , 45% aggregrate  is required.                           
  • Final year students are also eligible to apply for this course, provided that they provide the proof of completion of their graduation within the time period as given by the college.

Admissions:

In order to get admission in to full-time MBA course , students have to appear for MBA Entrance Exams.  MBA admissions are done on the basis of the percentile secured in this exam conducted at national or university level. 

To adhere to the industry needs and for students who wish to study while working, various other versions of MBA have been introduced . Some of them are Online MBA , Distance Learning and Executive MBA.

MCA Entrance Exams :

  • National Level Test conducted by an apex testing body or a top national B-school on behalf of the other participating colleges. eg: CAT, MAT, CMAT or ATMA.
  • State Level Test conducted by a state level testing body or a top state B-school on behalf of the other participating colleges in that state. eg: MAH-CET, OJEE, KMAT, TANCET or APICET.
  • Test conducted by a B-school for entrance to its own MBA programme. In some cases, these scores can be accepted as a qualifying criteria by other B-schools as well. eg: IIFT, XAT, NMAT, SNAP, IBSAT.
  • Test conducted by a university for entrance to MBA programmes being offered by colleges that are affiliated to it. eg: KIITEE, HPU MAT.

Course Curriculum :

Master of Business Administration is a two – year course. The coursework includes theory classes, practical projects, and internships.

The MBA course curriculum is a combination of some core subjects like Marketing, Economics, Accounting and Operations, and Elective courses to give management aspirants a chance to follow their own personal or professional interests.
